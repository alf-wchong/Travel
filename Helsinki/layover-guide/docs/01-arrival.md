# 🎯 Mission

Your goal is simple:

✅ Get through arrivals.

✅ Store your luggage.

✅ Reach the train station.

✅ Leave the airport stress-free.

If everything goes normally, you can accomplish all of that in **30–60 minutes** after collecting your luggage.

---

# 🚀 The 30-Second Version

Too tired to read?

Do this.

1. 🛄 Collect your luggage.
2. 🛂 Clear Customs.
3. 🚶 Walk into the Arrivals Hall.
4. 🟢 Find the **Alepa** supermarket.
5. 🚆 Follow the **Railway Station / Trains** signs.
6. ⬇️ Go down one level.
7. 🧳 Store your luggage.
8. 💳 Use your contactless card for the train.
9. 🚆 Board the next train to Helsinki Central Station.
10. 😎 Enjoy your layover.

That's it.

Everything else in this guide simply explains those ten steps.

# 🎯 Can You Actually Leave the Airport?

Most people can.

Here's a simple rule.

| ⏱️ Layover    | Recommendation        |
| :------------ | :-------------------- |
| Under 6 hours | ❌ Stay at the airport |
| 6–8 hours     | ⚠️ Maybe              |
| 8–10 hours    | ✅ Short visit         |
| 10–14 hours   | ✅ Highly recommended  |
| 14+ hours     | 😎 Plenty of time     |

This guide assumes you're in the **10–14 hour** category.

---

# 🏢 Understanding Helsinki Airport

One of the nicest things about Helsinki Airport is that it's compact.

Unlike airports where you might walk several kilometres between terminals, Helsinki keeps nearly everything you'll need within a short walk.

You'll mainly use:

* 🛄 Arrivals Hall
* 🟢 Alepa supermarket
* 🏧 ATMs
* 🚆 Railway Station
* 🧳 Luggage Storage
* ℹ️ Information Desk

Walking between any of these usually takes only a few minutes.

# 🚶 Step 1 — Exit Baggage Claim

🕒 Expected Time: 2–5 minutes

Once you've collected your bags:

➡️ Walk toward **Customs**.

Proceed through Customs into the public Arrivals Hall.

Don't overthink it.

There's essentially one obvious path.

---

## 👀 What Should You See?

When you enter the Arrivals Hall, expect to see:

☕ Cafés

👨‍👩‍👧 Families waiting

📺 Flight information displays

🧳 Travelers pulling luggage

🛒 Shops

If you see these...

✅ You're exactly where you should be.

---

> [!TIP]
> 📸 If you're meeting someone later in the city, now is a good time to send them a quick message while you still have airport Wi-Fi.

---

# 🚶 Step 2 — Find Alepa

🕒 Expected Time: 1 minute

Now comes the easiest navigation tip in the entire guide.

## 👀 Look For This

🟢 **Alepa**

Alepa is a supermarket inside the Arrivals Hall.

It has bright green branding and is difficult to miss.

Think of it as your first waypoint.

Once you've found Alepa...

You're correctly oriented.

---

## 🤔 Why Alepa?

Because people are terrible at remembering airport layouts...

...but very good at remembering a supermarket.

Whenever you become unsure of your location, ask yourself:

> **"Can I still find Alepa?"**

If yes...

You're still on course.

---

> [!NOTE]
> 🛒 Alepa is also a convenient place to buy snacks, drinks, or other essentials before heading into the city. Prices are generally more reasonable than many airport convenience stores.

---

# 🎯 Airport Brain Tip

Don't buy a huge bottle of water.

Finland has excellent drinking water.

You'll find refill points throughout the airport and city.

Save your money for coffee and cinnamon buns instead. ☕🥮

---

# 🚶 Step 3 — Follow the Railway Station Signs

🕒 **Expected Time:** 2–3 minutes

Now that you've found **🟢 Alepa**, it's time to head for the train station.

## 👀 Look For These Signs

Ignore the shop signs.

Ignore the restaurant signs.

Instead, look overhead for signs that say:

* 🚆 **Railway Station**
* 🚉 **Trains**
* 🚗 **Parking** (sometimes shown with a 🅿️ symbol)

These signs all point in roughly the same direction.

Your job is simple:

➡️ **Keep following the Railway Station signs.**

---

## 🎯 Success Looks Like This

Within a minute or two, you should see either:

* ⬇️ an escalator
* 🛗 an elevator

leading down toward the railway station.

If you see either one...

✅ You're exactly where you should be.

---

> [!TIP]
> 🚆 If you're ever unsure, don't look at the floor. Look **up**. Helsinki Airport's overhead wayfinding signs are excellent and are the fastest way to navigate.

---

# ⬇️ Step 4 — Go Down One Level

🕒 **Expected Time:** 1 minute

Take whichever is easiest:

* 🛗 Elevator
* ⬇️ Escalator

There is no "wrong" choice.

Everything remains inside the terminal.

You're simply moving from the arrivals level down to the transport level.

---

## 👀 What Changes?

You'll notice the atmosphere changes slightly.

Upstairs feels like an airport terminal.

Downstairs feels more like a modern train station.

Expect to see:

🚆 Train platforms

🚶 Commuters

🚉 Ticket information

🧳 Luggage storage

Concrete walls and transport corridors

This is normal.

---

# 🧳 Step 5 — Store Your Luggage

🕒 **Expected Time:** 5–10 minutes

Congratulations.

The hardest part is over.

Now decide how you'd like to store your luggage.

There are two options.

---

# 🅰️ Option A — Self-Service Lockers

**Best for most travelers.**

Perfect for:

🎒 Backpacks

💼 Laptop bags

🧳 Cabin luggage

🧳 Most medium and large suitcases

---

## 👍 Advantages

✅ Available whenever the locker area is open

✅ Fast

✅ No waiting for staff

✅ Secure

✅ Easy electronic payment

---

Most lockers accept:

# 💳 Do You Need Cash?

Short answer?

**Probably not.**

Finland is one of the most cashless countries in Europe.

During a normal day in Helsinki you can comfortably pay for almost everything with:

💳 Visa

💳 Mastercard

📱 Apple Pay

📱 Google Pay

⌚ Smartwatch payments

Many visitors never touch cash during their entire stay.

---

# 🏧 Should You Visit an ATM?

Only if you actually need euros.

Reasons you might use one:

✅ Your destination requires cash.

✅ You simply like carrying some local currency.

✅ Your bank card doesn't support contactless payments.

Otherwise...

Skip it.

---

## 👀 Where Are the ATMs?

You'll pass ATMs naturally while walking through the Arrivals Hall.

No detour is necessary.

If you don't immediately see one...

Don't worry.

You'll almost certainly encounter another before reaching the train station.

---

> [!TIP]
> 🏧 When using an ATM, choose to be charged in **euros** rather than your home currency if your bank offers competitive exchange rates. This often avoids less favorable dynamic currency conversion offered by the ATM.

---

# 💱 Should You Exchange Currency?

For most travelers...

❌ No.

Airport exchange counters are convenient, but they rarely offer the best exchange rates.

Using your card directly is usually the simpler and less expensive option.

---

# 🚰 Drinking Water

Good news.

Finnish tap water is excellent.

Seriously.

If you brought a reusable bottle:

💧 Fill it.

It's clean, safe, and tastes great.

There's little reason to spend money on bottled water unless you prefer sparkling water.

---

# 🔋 Charge Your Phone Before Leaving

This sounds obvious...

...until you're trying to navigate downtown Helsinki with 12% battery.

If your battery is below **50%**:

🔋 Stop.

🔌 Find a charging point.

☕ Grab a coffee.

📱 Leave with a full battery.

Your phone is now your:

🗺️ Map

🎫 Train ticket (if using the HSL app)

📸 Camera

💳 Payment method

✈️ Boarding pass

Treat it accordingly.

Don't worry.
